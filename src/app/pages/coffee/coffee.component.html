Espresso

Espresso is a concentrated coffee brewed by forcing hot water under high pressure through finely-ground coffee beans. It forms the base for many other coffee drinks.

Preparation:

  1. Grind coffee beans to a fine consistency.
  2. Fill the espresso machine's portafilter with coffee grounds.
  3. Tamp the grounds down firmly.
  4. Insert the portafilter into the espresso machine.
  5. Turn on the machine and wait for the espresso to brew.

Texture and flavour:

Espresso has a rich, bold flavour and a thick, creamy texture. It is the base for many other coffee drinks, such as lattes, cappuccinos, and macchiatos.

Ingredients:

  • Freshly ground coffee
  • Hot water

Latte

A latte (short for caff√® latte) is a creamy coffee drink made with a shot of espresso and steamed milk, topped with a thin layer of foam.

Preparation:

  1. Brew a shot of espresso.
  2. Steam milk until frothy.
  3. Pour the steamed milk into the espresso.
  4. Top with a thin layer of milk foam.

Texture and flavour:

A latte has a smooth, creamy texture and a mild coffee flavour. The steamed milk adds a touch of sweetness and richness to the drink.

Ingredients:

  • Espresso
  • Steamed milk
  • Foamed milk

Cappuccino

A balanced coffee drink with equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and foam.

Preparation:

  1. Brew a shot of espresso.
  2. Steam milk until frothy.
  3. Pour the steamed milk into the espresso.
  4. Top with a thick layer of milk foam.

Texture and flavour:

A cappuccino has a velvety texture and a strong coffee flavour. The milk foam adds a light, airy quality to the drink.

Ingredients:

  • Espresso
  • Steamed milk
  • Milk foam
